I

Overview

A competitive engine-building and battle game set in mythological Greece. Build your divine engine, outmanoeuvre your rivals, and earn the most Patron Points to claim victory in the great Agon.

Players summon Spirits, recruit Demi-Gods, call upon the twelve Olympians, unleash Monsters, and forge Weapons. Every God, Demi-God, and Monster card exists as a single copy. Once someone acquires it, it is locked in their engine for the rest of the game. That scarcity is the spine of every decision.

Objective

The player with the most Patron Points (PP) when the final round ends wins. PP comes from converting resources at the Oracle, completing quest cards, winning battles, scoring round goals, and the end-game value of your engine. Track your PP on the shared score track or with PP tokens.

II

Laps & Rounds

Why Laps Exist

A round is divided into laps. In each lap, every player takes one full turn (2 actions). After all laps are complete, the round goal is scored and the round ends. Laps give the engine time to compound, allow players room to recover, and make the game reactive rather than a sequence of independent turns with no interplay.

Lap Order

Turns alternate within each lap. The player holding the first-player marker goes first in every lap of that round. Example for 2 players with 3 laps:

Lap 1: Player 1 (2 actions) → Player 2 (2 actions)

Lap 2: Player 1 (2 actions) → Player 2 (2 actions)

Lap 3: Player 1 (2 actions) → Player 2 (2 actions)

End of Round: Score the goal. Pass first-player marker clockwise.

Per Round vs. On Your Turn

Clarification

Per Round — fires exactly once at Step 1 (engine phase), before any laps begin. A 3-lap round still triggers this only once.

On Your Turn — fires during your individual 2-action turn. In a 3-lap round, "on your turn" effects can fire up to 3 times per player. When in doubt: did this text say "per round" or did it say "on your turn"? That distinction is definitive.

Full Round Sequence

1
Engines Fire

All players simultaneously collect resources from every card in their engine. Happens once before any laps begin.

2
Monster Afflictions Fire

Every deployed monster's effect triggers once for its target. Targets may pay the removal cost here as a free action before their first lap.

3
Goal Reveal

If the round goal is not yet visible, flip it face-up now. All players see the objective.

4
Laps

Players alternate turns within each lap for the correct number of laps. Each turn is 2 actions. Free actions are available at any point during your own turn.

5
Score the Goal

After the final lap, resolve the round goal. The qualifying player earns the PP. Apply the tie rule if needed. Discard the goal card.

6
Pass First Player

The marker moves clockwise. Omen Tokens, Glory Track positions, and all engine cards carry over to the next round.

III

Resources

🟡
Ambrosia

The common currency. Acquired at Olympus, from Spirits, and many card effects. Used to buy cards, pay costs, and convert at the Oracle.

2 Ambrosia = 1 PP
🔵
Nectar

The premium resource. More valuable at the Oracle. Primarily generated by God cards and sea-domain Spirit cards.

1 Nectar = 2 PP
🔴
Ichor

Divine blood. Rare. Required to deploy monsters, summon Gods, forge certain weapons, and activate specific powers. You start with none.

1 Ichor = 3 PP
⚑ Critical Note

Ichor scarcity is intentional. It only comes from specific card passives once your engine is built. Plan your Ichor engine early — without it you cannot summon Gods or deploy the strongest Monsters.

Resource Conversion

Resources are converted to Patron Points at Apollo's Oracle Fire domain during your turn. You may mix resource types freely in a single visit. There is no per-turn conversion limit — spend as much as you can afford. Engine 🔱 cards add +1 PP per Oracle visit on top of conversion rates.

Omen Tokens

You gain 1 Omen Token per turn in which you visit the Oracle (not per Oracle visit within a turn). At game end, every 3 Omen Tokens you hold are worth +2 PP. Omen Tokens are not a resource — they cannot be spent or converted mid-game.

IV

The Five Domains

Each turn you choose 2 actions from the five domains. You may visit the same domain twice in one turn. Domain Mastery (most visits at game end) earns bonus PP — see Scoring.

🌑
Hades' Eternal Dark
"Descend with Charon"

Pay a card's cost to acquire any face-up card from the Underworld market. The market always shows 4 Spirits, 2 Demi-Gods, 2 Monsters, 1 Weapon. Refills immediately after each purchase.

To summon a God: pay the God's Ichor cost, reveal the top 2 God cards, keep 1, return the other to the bottom of the God deck.

⛔ Blocked while Cerberus is deployed on you.

⚡
Zeus' Eternal Throne
"Receive Divine Favour"

Choose one: 2 Ambrosia, 1 Nectar, or 1 Ichor. Each ⚡ card in your engine adds +1 Ambrosia when you choose the Ambrosia option (bonuses do not apply to Nectar or Ichor choices).

⚖ v6 Balance — Zeus in Engine Once per round (not per lap, not per opponent visit), the Zeus card owner may reduce one chosen opponent's Ambrosia gain at this domain by 1 (minimum 1 Ambrosia total). The owner must declare this before the target resolves their Olympus action. The same opponent cannot be targeted two rounds in a row.
🔥
Apollo's Oracle Fire
"Consult the Oracle"

Convert any combination of resources to Patron Points:

2 Ambrosia = 1 PP  ·  1 Nectar = 2 PP  ·  1 Ichor = 3 PP

Each 🔱 card in your engine adds +1 PP per visit. Gain 1 Omen Token this turn (once per turn regardless of how many Oracle visits you make).

⛔ Blocked while the Sphinx is deployed on you.

🔨
Hephaestus' Forge
"Commission the Smith"

Pay a weapon's cost to acquire it from the Forge market. Choose immediately: equip it (move to engine for its passive bonus) or hold it in hand (for battle play or one-time activation later). The Forge always shows 3 Weapons, refilling instantly.

If the Weapon deck is exhausted, visiting the Forge gives 1 Ambrosia instead.

⚔️
Ares' Blood Colosseum
"Enter the Fray"

Choose one of the following:

a) Deploy a Monster — pay 1 Ichor (2 for Typhon), place it face-up in front of any opponent with fewer than 2 deployed monsters.

b) Challenge an opponent to a battle (see Battle System).

c) Activate a weapon's one-time ability (see Weapon Rules).

Maximum 2 monsters on any one player at a time.

⛔ Aphrodite protects one chosen opponent per round from being targeted here.

V

Your Turn

Take 2 actions in any combination. You may visit the same domain twice.

Actions

  • 🌑
    Hades — Acquire a Card
    Pay the card's printed cost. Take it into your hand. The market refills instantly. To summon a God, pay its Ichor cost: reveal the top 2 God cards, keep 1, return the other to the bottom of the deck.
  • ⚡
    Olympus — Gain Resources
    Choose: 2 Ambrosia, 1 Nectar, or 1 Ichor. ⚡ engine cards add +1 Ambrosia when you choose Ambrosia. Zeus' active bonus applies as per v6 rule (once per round, one opponent, no consecutive targeting).
  • ▶
    Play a Card — Build Your Engine
    Move any card from your hand to your engine. Trigger its On Play effect immediately. Starting from the next round's engine phase, it produces automatically. No additional cost to play.
  • 🔥
    Oracle's Fire — Score PP
    Convert resources to PP at the Oracle rates (2 Amb = 1 PP · 1 Nec = 2 PP · 1 Ich = 3 PP). 🔱 engine cards add +1 PP per visit. Gain 1 Omen Token this turn (once per turn, not per visit).
  • 🔨
    Forge — Acquire a Weapon
    Pay the weapon's cost. Take it from the Forge. Immediately decide: equip it to your engine (passive bonus from next engine phase) or keep it in hand (for battle or activation).
  • ⚔️
    Colosseum — Deploy, Battle, or Activate
    Deploy a Monster (1 Ichor; 2 for Typhon), challenge an opponent to battle, or activate a weapon's one-time ability. Only one of these per Colosseum action. See respective sections for full rules.

Free Actions

These cost no action slot. Use them at any point during your own turn only.

  • 📜
    Complete a Quest
    Reveal a quest card from your hand when its condition is met. Collect the PP reward immediately. Limit: 2 completed quests per player per game.
  • ✕
    Emergency Discard
    Discard 1 card from your hand and take 1 Ambrosia from the supply. Exception: the Phoenix returns to your hand instead of the discard pile.
  • 🛡
    Remove a Monster
    Pay the removal cost printed on a monster deployed on you. Claim the card as a trophy — its printed PP value counts at game end. The monster is permanently removed from the game.
  • 🏛
    Sanctuary (once per game)
    If you begin your turn with 0 resources and 0 cards in hand, you may declare Sanctuary. Gain 2 Ambrosia from the supply. Each player may use this only once per game.
VI

Weapons

Weapons are dual-purpose cards: they provide a passive bonus when equipped in your engine and can also be played in battles. Each weapon type has 2 copies — two players may hold the same weapon simultaneously.

Two States

StateHow to EnterWhat It Does
Equipped (in engine)Use a Play Card action to move it from hand to engine.Gives passive bonus each engine phase. Can still be played in battle (returns to engine after).
In HandPurchased from Forge and held back, or drawn at setup.No passive bonus. Can be played face-down in a battle (returns to hand after) or activated for its one-time ability (then discarded).

Activating a Weapon

Using a weapon's one-time ability is your Colosseum action for that turn. You may activate from hand or from your engine. Either way, the weapon is discarded after use. You cannot activate a weapon that you already played in a battle on the same turn.

⚖ v6 Balance — Weapon Damage Cap

Combined weapon bonuses in a single battle are capped at +4 total (the sum of both weapons played, across both sides combined). Any weapon effect that would push the combined total above +4 is treated as +4. This prevents weapon stacking from making battles feel predetermined.

Dual Auto-Win Rule

If both players play a weapon with an auto-win effect in the same battle, treat the result as a tie. Both players lose 1 Ambrosia. Neither weapon is discarded — both return to their previous state (engine or hand).

Weapon End-Game Value

Weapons equipped in your engine at game end are worth 1–3 PP each (printed on the card). Weapons held in hand at game end have no PP value.

VII

Battle System

Initiating a battle is a Colosseum action. Battles cannot target a player who is protected by Aphrodite this round. God and Demi-God cards in engines cannot be targeted or stolen — only resources and direct effects.

Battle Sequence

1
Declare a Target

Name the opponent you are challenging. They must not have Aphrodite's protection this round.

2
Choose Weapons (Secret)

Both players simultaneously and secretly choose 0 or 1 weapon from their hand or engine. Do not reveal until both have chosen.

3
Reveal & Calculate

Reveal simultaneously. Attacker base: 2. Defender base: 1. Add weapon bonuses from both sides. Combined weapon bonus cap: +4 total. Higher final total wins.

4
Resolve Outcome

Apply the result below. Any weapon played in battle returns to its previous state unless the dual auto-win tie applies.

Outcomes

Attacker Wins: Steal 2 Ambrosia from the defender. Gain 1 PP. Advance 1 on the Glory Track.

Defender Wins: Defender gains 1 PP. Attacker loses 1 Ambrosia to the supply (not to the defender).

Tie: Both players lose 1 Ambrosia to the supply. No PP is awarded to either player.

The Glory Track

Glory ScorePP at Game EndHow to Earn Glory
0 – 2+0 PP+1 per battle won · +1 per quest completed
3 – 4+2 PP+1 per monster deployed that survives 2+ rounds
5 – 6+4 PPHeracles, Atalanta, and Ares engine bonuses each add +1 extra Glory per trigger
7++6 PPDionysus' Divine Madness or Zeus' Storm's Wrath awards +2 Glory when used

Glory Track position is public. It carries over between rounds and is only ever increased, never decreased. It is one of the game's tiebreakers.

VIII

Monsters

Every Monster card is unique: one copy, consumed on use, permanently removed from the game. Monsters are the game's primary disruption tool — powerful but irreversible.

Two Ways to Use a Monster

MethodCostEffectCounter?
Instant UseColosseum action only (no Ichor)Play from hand. Instant effect fires immediately. Card is discarded.Cannot be countered.
DeployColosseum action + 1 Ichor (2 for Typhon)Place face-up in front of any opponent. Apply a duration token. Effect fires once per round at Step 2.Can be removed by target (they gain trophy).

Deploy Effect Timing

Deployed monster effects fire once per round at Step 2, before any laps begin. They do not fire again during individual turns. A 2-round monster fires twice total: once at Step 2 of the round it was deployed, and once at Step 2 of the following round.

⚑ Global Timing Rule

"Fires once per round at Step 2" applies to every monster in the game. There is no monster whose deploy effect fires per-turn. This is the universal standard — no exceptions.

Removal & Trophies

The target may pay the printed removal cost during Step 2 (before their first lap of the round) or at any point during their own turn as a free action. Upon paying, they claim the monster card as a trophy. Trophies count for their printed PP value at game end.

Natural Expiry

When a monster's duration runs out without the target paying the removal cost, the monster card is discarded permanently. No trophy is awarded. The monster is gone.

Monster Safety Rules

Two-Monster Cap: A maximum of 2 monsters may be deployed on any single player at a time. Deploying a third requires removing one of the existing two first — it expires immediately with no trophy for anyone.

No Stacking Same-Type Restrictions: Two monsters that impose the same type of restriction (e.g., both block domain access) do not stack. Only the one with the longer remaining duration is active.

Sanctuary: If a player begins their turn with 0 resources and 0 cards in hand, they may declare Sanctuary once per game — gain 2 Ambrosia from the supply as a free action.

IX

All Cards — 60 Unique Types

The full card roster breaks into five categories. Cards marked Unique exist as a single copy in the game — once acquired, they cannot be obtained by any other player.

Spirits (20 types · 4 copies each)

Spirits are the backbone of every engine. They produce resources during the engine phase, add bonuses at specific domains, and chain with God cards. Each Spirit bears a domain icon (⚡ or 🔱 or other) that determines which engine bonuses it unlocks. Multiple players may hold identical Spirits.

Demi-Gods (8 types · 1 copy each) Unique

Demi-Gods are high-value engine anchors acquired from the Hades market. Each has an On Play effect and a passive engine bonus. Their unique status makes them contested draft targets. They count for 2–4 PP at game end.

Notable Demi-GodsRole
HeraclesBattle-focused. Engine bonus: +1 Glory per triggered battle ability. Also grants +1 extra Glory to Glory Track triggers.
AtalantaSpeed and quests. Engine bonus: +1 Glory per quest completed. Complements Athena's quest synergy.
Others (6)Details printed on cards. Each has a unique engine passive and On Play effect worth 2–4 PP at game end.

Gods (12 types · 1 copy each) Unique

The twelve Olympians are summoned from a face-down deck by paying Ichor at Hades' domain. They are the most powerful engine cards — each with a passive and an active ability. All count for 2–4 PP at game end.

⚖ v6 Balance — God & Demi-God PP Cap

No single God or Demi-God card may provide more than 4 PP of direct passive income per round. Effects exceeding this must be converted to conditional bonuses (e.g., "if you control 3+ ⚡ cards") or split between engine-phase and on-play one-time effects. This keeps unique-card power exciting without making them insurmountable once obtained.

X

Quest Cards

15 unique quest types · 2 copies each (30 total). Quests represent deeds accomplished during the Agon. They reward 2–5 PP each and interact with the Glory Track and card synergies.

Setup

Shuffle the quest deck. Deal 3 quest cards to each player face-down. Each player looks at their 3 cards privately, keeps 2, and returns 1 face-down to the bottom of the quest deck. Your kept quests remain secret until you reveal one.

Completing a Quest

During your turn, when the printed condition on one of your quest cards is met, reveal it as a free action. Collect the PP reward immediately. The card is set aside face-up. You are limited to completing 2 quests per player per game.

Quest Synergies

Athena (God card): +1 bonus PP for every quest you complete.

Atalanta (Demi-God card): +1 Glory per quest completed.

Glory Track: Completing a quest earns +1 Glory regardless of its PP reward.

XI

Round Goals

8 unique round goal cards · 1 copy each. One goal is revealed per round. The qualifying player earns PP after the final lap of that round. Goals are shuffled at the start of the game, so the full order is unknown to all players — strategic planning works within each round, not across the whole game.

Scoring a Round Goal

After the final lap of a round, check the goal condition. The player who best meets the condition wins the PP. If tied, consult the tie rule printed on the card (typically: most Glory, then most Omen Tokens). Discard the goal card — it does not reenter play.

⚖ v6 Balance — Round Goal Ceiling

No single round goal may award more than 5 PP. Goals that depend on cumulative engine size (e.g., "most cards in engine," "most resources held") award only 3 PP to reduce their snowball potential for players already in the lead.

XII

Scoring

PP is accumulated throughout the game. At the end of the final round, add all remaining PP sources together. The player with the highest total wins.

🔥
Oracle's Fire

2 Amb = 1 PP · 1 Nec = 2 PP · 1 Ich = 3 PP. Scored continuously during play.

🏆
Round Goals

3–5 PP per round. 5–6 rounds means up to 25–30 PP from goals alone.

📜
Quest Cards

2–5 PP each. Up to 2 quests per player. Athena adds +1 PP per quest.

✨
Card End Values

Spirits: 1–2 PP · Demi-Gods: 2–4 PP · Gods: 2–4 PP. Counted at game end.

⚔️
Battle Wins

+1 PP per Colosseum battle won (in addition to the 2 Ambrosia stolen).

🗡️
Weapons in Engine

1–3 PP per equipped weapon at game end. Weapons in hand have no end value.

🌟
Glory Track

+2 PP at 3–4 · +4 PP at 5–6 · +6 PP at 7+. Position never decreases.

🔮
Omen Tokens

+2 PP per 3 tokens held. Max 1 token gained per turn (not per Oracle visit).

🏛️
Domain Mastery

+2 PP per dominated domain. Cap: max 3 domains per player. See note below.

👁️
Monster Trophies

Each trophy card counts for its printed PP value at game end.

⚖ v6 Balance — Domain Mastery Cap

A single player may claim a maximum of 3 domains at game end, regardless of actual visit counts. If a player has the most visits in 4 or 5 domains, they still only score the top 3. The 4th and 5th most-visited domains are awarded to the player with the next highest visit count in those domains. This prevents a "domain sweep" strategy from dominating and keeps all scoring paths competitive.

Tiebreakers

If two or more players are tied on PP at game end, resolve in this order:

  1. Most total resources held (Ambrosia + Nectar + Ichor combined).
  2. Most God cards in engine.
  3. Highest Glory Track position.
  4. Most Omen Tokens remaining.
XIII

Setup

Estimated setup time: 10 minutes. Follow these steps in order before Round 1 begins.

1
Place the Board

Lay out the game board. Identify the five domain areas, the Glory Track, Domain Mastery track, Omen Token area, and the shared score track (0–75).

2
Hades Market

Shuffle the Spirit, Demi-God, and Monster decks separately. Reveal 4 Spirits, 2 Demi-Gods, and 2 Monsters face-up in the Underworld market. Place the Weapon from the Hades market slot (1 Weapon) face-up too.

3
Forge Market

Shuffle the Weapon deck. Reveal 3 Weapons face-up in the Forge. Return the Weapon deck near the Forge for refills.

4
God Deck

Shuffle all 12 God cards face-down into a single God deck. Place it near Hades' domain. Gods are not placed in the market — they are summoned when a player pays Ichor at Hades.

5
Round Goal Deck

Shuffle all 8 Round Goal cards face-down. Place as a deck. Reveal the top card face-up — this is Round 1's goal. Players see it before any turns are taken.

6
Quest Cards

Shuffle the quest deck (30 cards). Deal 3 quest cards to each player face-down. Each player privately keeps 2 and returns 1 face-down to the bottom of the quest deck.

7
Starting Resources & Cards

Give each player: 4 Ambrosia, 1 Nectar, and deal 2 Spirit cards from the top of the Spirit deck into each player's hand (not from the market). All players start at position 0 on the Glory Track with 0 Domain Visit cubes placed.

8
First Player & Begin

The youngest player takes the first-player marker (olive wreath token). They go first in Lap 1 of Round 1. The Agon begins.
